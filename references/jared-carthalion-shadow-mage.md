# Jared Carthalion, the Shadow Mage

Jared Carthalion is an early Dominaria-era Magic character whose story begins in ruin, passes through exile, betrayal, and apprenticeship, and culminates in planeswalker ascension during the crisis around Ravidel and the Mox Beacon.  He matters because he sits at the intersection of old comic-era Magic lore, the symbolic weight of the Black Lotus, the five Moxes, and a hard-earned apotheosis rather than an effortless rise to power.

## Canon status

Much of Jared's core life story comes from the 1995 Armada comics *The Shadow Mage* and *Wayfarer*, and the MTG Wiki notes that stories from that early period are of uncertain relevance under later canon standards.  That means the broad outline of Jared's life is well preserved in lore summaries, but fine-grained details from those comics should be treated as early-canon material rather than perfectly harmonized modern continuity.  His later reappearance in *The Education of Ulf* confirms that Jared exists in modern published Magic story as an old hero still acting on Dominaria.

## Bloodline and birth

Jared is a human from Corondor, born into the Carthalion line, a family with deep roots stretching back into Dominaria's older history.  He was born in autumn 3964 AR, the son of Adam and Gwendolyn Carthalion, and later becomes the father of Justina Carthalion.  His life is framed from the start by inheritance, but the point of the story is that inheritance alone does not save him.

## The catastrophe of infancy

Jared's origin is bound to his father Adam's dealings with Ravidel, the planeswalker-battlemage who manipulated Adam's ambitions and pushed him toward sacrificial crimes in the name of gaining power.  In the comics and summary lore, Adam sacrificed Gwendolyn and nearly sacrificed Jared as well, but refused at the last moment, turned against Ravidel, and died in that rebellion.  Jared was struck by a soul-draining effect during this conflict, leaving him physically altered and unrecognizable as the rightful Carthalion heir.

## Beggar prince

After the fall of his house, Jared was raised by Ezer, his father's spellsquire, and grew up not as a lord but as a beggar in the streets of Arathoxia.  This is one of the defining facts of his biography because it makes Jared's story fundamentally anti-aristocratic: he is a true heir who grows up dispossessed, beaten, hungry, and hidden in the city that should have been his.  The comics repeatedly frame him among thieves, street children, and collapsing shelter rather than in halls of power.

## Why he is called the Shadow Mage

In *The Shadow Mage*, Jared first reveals dangerous magical potential as a child while defending himself and Ezer from one of Ravidel's attacks, and Ezer names him "the Shadow Mage" after witnessing that awakening.  The title does not mean he is merely a master of black mana or stealth magic in a narrow rules sense; it marks him as someone shaped by concealment, marginalization, and survival in the shadows of greater powers.  As a character label, "Shadow Mage" captures both obscurity and latent force.

## Arathoxia and the making of character

By adolescence Jared is physically hardened, suspicious, and reluctant to trust power, including his own magic, because every contact with power in his life has been contaminated by manipulation or violence.  Even so, the story shows him repeatedly choosing restraint at critical moments, such as pulling back summoned violence when it would cross a moral line, which distinguishes him from Ravidel's appetite for domination.  This moral hesitation matters because Jared's ascent is presented as earned through discipline and suffering, not merely unlocked by bloodline.

## Foecleaver

Jared's signature weapon is Foecleaver, a sword tied to his father and later described in modern summary as a heavy blade he can wield in one hand, sometimes channeling flame through it.  The sword symbolizes continuity with the Carthalion line, but Jared's use of it is practical rather than ceremonial.  He is not just a wizard or just a warrior. He is consistently framed as both swordsman and mage.

## Liana of Minorad

Liana is one of the pivotal figures in Jared's youth, first appearing as a hidden watcher, later becoming an ally, rescuer, and eventually a tragic casualty of the war around Ravidel.  She possesses Moxes of her own in the comic material, which immediately links Jared's orbit to some of the most symbolically loaded artifacts in early Magic lore.  Liana also embodies one of the recurring themes of Jared's life: important truth is always partial, delayed, or entangled with oath, deception, and divided loyalty.

## The Black Lotus

The Black Lotus enters Jared's story through the Scarlet estate in Arathoxia, where he steals it during his desperate effort to save Ezer and survive Ravidel's net of control.  In the climax at Castle Melmereth, Jared crushes the Lotus and summons a Shivan Dragon, unleashing the force that destroys the castle and appears to kill Ravidel.  Within Jared's biography, the Black Lotus is not just a famous card cameo. It is the instrument of decisive rupture, the moment concentrated power is converted into irreversible action.

## Extra effort on the Lotus

Outside Jared's specific biography, the Black Lotus is part of Magic's Power Nine and is widely regarded as the game's most iconic and explosively efficient mana artifact.  That broader symbolic reputation intensifies its role in Jared's story because the Lotus is not random treasure. It is the franchise's most condensed emblem of sudden, game-changing power.  In narrative terms, the Lotus lets Jared do what his life had not yet allowed him to do normally: force history to move in one violent stroke.

## The Moxes

The Moxes are the five original zero-cost mana artifacts tied to the five colors of Magic, and they belong to the same symbolic class as the Black Lotus within the Power Nine.  Jared's lore intersects with them most clearly through Liana's possession of multiple Moxes and later through Jared escaping with the Moxes and Kristina during the escalating conflict with Ravidel.  The Moxes in his story are not mere collectibles. They are markers of color mastery, planeswalker-level conflict, and the widening scale of Jared's destiny.

## Extra effort on the Moxes

The MTG Wiki's Mox overview establishes the Moxes as color-defined mana accelerants, which makes them ideal narrative shorthand for mastery over distinct magical principles rather than generic power.  Jared's apotheosis later hinges on drawing on all five colors of mana, so the story's connection between Jared, Liana's Moxes, and the Mox Beacon creates a coherent symbolic arc from fragmented color-power to total integration.  In other words, the Moxes matter in Jared's legend because they point toward the final form of his power long before he fully attains it.

## Wayfarer years

After the apparent fall of Ravidel at Melmereth, Jared leaves Liana and sets out to become a Wayfarer, a wandering seeker of magical growth rather than a static avenger.  During this phase he encounters Kadesha, Élan d'Avenant, and most importantly Kristina of the Woods, who begins teaching him how to use all aspects of mana.  This part of the story matters because it converts Jared from reactive survivor into disciplined student.

## Kristina of the Woods

Kristina is central to Jared's maturation, both emotionally and metaphysically.  The lore summary states that they fell in love and that she taught him to use all aspects of mana, which is a direct precondition for his later ascension.  If Ezer is the guardian of Jared's survival, Kristina is the architect of Jared's completion.

## The return of Ravidel

Jared and Kristina learn that Ravidel survived, and Jared is drawn back into confrontation by unfinished history rather than simple revenge fantasy.  During this phase Jared witnesses a duel between Ravidel and Liana, accidentally contributes to chaos that ends in Liana's death, and escapes with Kristina and the Moxes on the back of the Shivan Dragon.  The story becomes larger than Arathoxia at this point. It is no longer about one heir reclaiming one city, but about old planeswalkers, relics, and world-shaping engines.

## The Mox Beacon

Ravidel takes Liana's Moxen and uses them to erect the Mox Beacon, a device associated with initiating the Planeswalker War.  This is one of the most important transitions in Jared's life because the Moxes move from being potent artifacts in private hands to being components in a world-historic planeswalker conflict.  Jared's story is therefore not just adjacent to the Moxes. It is entangled with one of the major attempts to weaponize them at scale.

## The road to apotheosis

Before his final ascent, Jared travels to Corundis, acquires long-daggers used in the destruction of the Golgothian Sylex, and gathers allies including Grenfell Mor, Altair of Coloni, Caliphear the Nightmare, and Kristina.  These episodes mark the movement from personal vendetta to coalition struggle, and from improvised survival to prepared confrontation.  Jared's growth is cumulative. Every companion contributes some piece of the whole person he becomes.

## The battle at the Dueling Chasm of Golthonor

Jared's decisive confrontation with Ravidel occurs at the Dueling Chasm of Golthonor, where the Mox Beacon is activated amid catastrophic instability.  During this battle Jared is able to draw on all five colors of mana, a capacity the lore explicitly attributes to the instruction he received from Kristina and other companions.  This is the hinge of his life: he ceases to be a talented survivor and becomes something cosmically different.

## Apotheosis

Jared's apotheosis is his ascension into planeswalkerhood during the collapse at Golthonor, when he sparks and planeswalks away in time to survive the crumbling chasm and save Kristina.  The lore presents this as a moment of integrated five-color mastery under extreme pressure, not as a convenient inheritance trigger.  Altair dies in the conflict, which preserves a key feature of old Magic ascension stories: transcendence does not cancel tragedy.

## Extra effort on apotheosis

Jared's ascent is unusual in emotional texture because it does not read like a coronation. It reads like a late ignition after years of mutilated identity, false history, scavenged education, and repeated contact with artifacts and powers larger than himself.  Thematically, his apotheosis is the answer to Ravidel's entire model of power. Ravidel seeks domination through sacrifice and instrumental cruelty, while Jared reaches planeswalker status through survival, relational teaching, and integrated discipline.  In structural terms, Jared does not become divine by discarding his wounded past. He becomes powerful by metabolizing it.

## After ascension

A year and a half after the battle, Jared and Kristina have a daughter, Justina Carthalion, indicating that his rise to planeswalker status does not remove him from personal life or lineage.  The MTG Wiki further notes that Jared was likely affected by the Great Mending like other oldwalkers, losing his immortality and much of his godlike scale.  That detail matters because it places his apotheosis within the oldwalker era and later subjects it to the franchise-wide redefinition of planeswalkers.

## The old hero in modern story

In *The Education of Ulf*, published for *Dominaria United*, Jared appears as an old man still carrying Foecleaver and acting in defense of innocents during the second Phyrexian invasion of Dominaria.  He is connected there to the effort to free Corondor from Geyadrone Dihada's influence, which ties his late-life action back to one of Dominaria's oldest corruptions.  This late appearance confirms that Jared's story is not only an archival curiosity from the comic era. He remains an active moral and historical presence in the setting.

## Symbols that define him

Several objects and motifs structure Jared's life: Foecleaver, the Black Lotus, the Moxes, the Shivan Dragon, the mark of druidic power on his face, and the five-color horizon of his eventual magic.  Together these symbols produce a consistent image: a man of broken inheritance who acquires power in fragments, carries weapons and relics burdened by history, and finally reaches completion through synthesis rather than purity.  That is why Jared remains memorable even though much of his story comes from early comics with uneven canonical standing.

## Relationships

The key relationships in Jared's life are Ezer, who preserves him; Liana, who entangles him with secret knowledge, rescue, and loss; Kristina, who teaches and completes him; Adam, whose failure deforms Jared's beginning; and Ravidel, who functions as both persecutor and dark mirror.  Each relationship changes Jared's access to truth and power. None of them are ornamental.  His biography is therefore relational before it is imperial.

## Themes

Jared's story is built on dispossession, hidden identity, apprenticeship, moral restraint, catastrophic power, and earned ascension.  The Black Lotus represents compressed decisive force, the Moxes represent color-differentiated mastery, and his apotheosis represents synthesis under pressure.  If Urza often reads as the architect of grand systems, Jared reads as the survivor who becomes equal to history only after history has tried to erase him.

## Firm lore vs interpretation

Firmly established in the sourced summaries are Jared's origin in the Carthalion line, his childhood in Arathoxia, his connection to Ezer, Liana, Kristina, the Black Lotus, the Moxes, Ravidel, the Mox Beacon, and his ascension at Golthonor while drawing on all five colors of mana.  Interpretive synthesis begins when describing the Black Lotus as "decisive rupture," the Moxes as "fragments of eventual totality," or Jared's apotheosis as the moral inversion of Ravidel's power model.  Those interpretations are faithful to the narrative pattern, but they are still interpretation rather than explicit quoted doctrine.

## Short archival profile

Jared Carthalion, the Shadow Mage, is a dispossessed heir of Dominaria who survives a corrupt origin, grows up as a beggar, claims the Black Lotus in a moment of desperation, passes through wandering apprenticeship, becomes entangled with the Moxes and the Mox Beacon, and finally ascends as a planeswalker by drawing on all five colors of mana.  His legend is strongest when read as a story of earned power, not inherited entitlement.
